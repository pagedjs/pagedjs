Chapter 1: Fractions

In this chapter, we'll explore the fundamental concepts of fractions, including addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.

1.1 Understanding Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole. It consists of two numbers: the numerator (top number) and the denominator (bottom number).

Key Concept

The denominator tells us how many equal parts the whole is divided into, while the numerator tells us how many of those parts we have.

1.2 Adding Fractions

When adding fractions with the same denominator, we add the numerators and keep the denominator the same. 

1/4 + 2/4 = (1 + 2)/4 = 3/4

Step 1: Check that denominators are the same
Step 2: Add the numerators: 1 + 2 = 3
Step 3: Keep the denominator: 4
Step 4: Simplify if needed: 3/4 (already simplified)

Chapter 2: Decimals

Decimals are another way to represent parts of a whole. In this chapter, we'll learn how decimals relate to fractions and how to perform operations with them.

Decimal Place Values

Place Value Example
Ones 1 5 in 5.234
Tenths 0.1 2 in 5.234
Hundredths 0.01 3 in 5.234
Thousandths 0.001 4 in 5.234
